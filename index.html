What are NFTs?

NFTs (non-fungible tokens) are singular bits of code and software that can store information. Unlike other cryptocurrency assets, NFTs are singular. That means they act as an individual file that can’t be replicated or combined with other crypto assets. In the Web3 world, NFTs can serve as certificates of authenticity or proof of ownership, giving way to a whole new wave of digital artists who can now take better ownership of their work and collectors who can verify their ownership. NFTs are metadata files and do not actually store the image/media file that they’re often known for but rather a link to it (usually hosted on the server of the creator or marketplace).
